## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### The strategic frame: HORIZONTAL, NOT VERTICAL

We are **not** a "design partner for fitness chains". We are not a "wellness specialist" or a "retail boutique". Our portfolio (Benefit Systems, Sonova, Kubota, Podsiadło, Archicom, Caterelo, DiscoBowl, Zdrofit, UNIQA, FIFA) deliberately spans verticals because **our value is structural, not industry-specific**.

The pattern across every client we've delivered for: **organizations with multi-X complexity** — where X is locations, stakeholders, brands, products, formats, or investments — that need this complexity *operationalized*, not just decorated.

### Who buys

**Role:**
- COO / CEO / Director of Operations / VP of Brand / Head of Strategy
- *Not* CMO of a single-brand consumer company. *Not* Head of Design. *Not* Procurement.
- Decision maker who pays personally for outcome, not for vendor name on a slide.

**Org profile — any of these patterns qualifies:**
- **Multi-location physical**: 20+ locations (fitness, wellness, health, retail, hospitality, F&B)
- **Multi-stakeholder platform**: HR-tech, B2B SaaS, employee benefits, marketplaces
- **Multi-brand portfolio**: holding companies, FMCG groups, fashion houses with sub-brands
- **Multi-product enterprise**: insurance, banking, telco with parallel product lines
- **Multi-investment portfolio**: real estate developers, PE-owned multi-asset operators
- **Multi-format identity**: artists, sports federations, major event organizers
- **Multi-channel commerce**: omnichannel retail, D2C+wholesale brands

**Common pain regardless of vertical:**
- "Our brand looks different in every [location / brand / channel / format]"
- "Marketing/brief requests are eating our internal team"
- "We just changed our agency. Again."
- "We can't get briefs out fast enough to keep up with openings/launches/campaigns"
- "Our regional/category/product managers are doing their own design"
- "The agency made it pretty but didn't make it work for our 40 operators"

**Revenue/budget reality:**
- Revenue: **€10M–€2B annually** (broad band because verticals scale differently)
- Solo decision authority: **€15k–€80k**
- With board escalation: **€80k–€300k+**
- Recurring monthly: **€3k–€12k retainer comfortable**

## 4. Decision Filters

Every meaningful decision must pass these tests:

### The 8-Second Test
Will a COO of a 40-location wellness chain understand what we sell within 8 seconds of landing on the page?

### The iPad Test
Does this work on iPad in landscape, in a board meeting, between two other tabs?

### The CFO-Defensible Test
If the COO has to justify this purchase to a CFO, is the pricing/scope/outcome defensible in one sentence?

### The Anti-Vendor Test
Does this position us as an **operating partner** or as an **estetic vendor**? Vendor language: "we craft", "we deliver", "we design". Partner language: "we operationalize", "we systemize", "we de-risk".

### The 1M PLN Test
Does this move us closer to 1M PLN/year, or is it polish? If polish: defer.

## 5. No-Go List

Things we do NOT do — even when tempting:

1. ❌ **RFP responses** unless invited by warm intro (procurement cycles burn 60h+ for 5% close rate)
2. ❌ **Free pitches / spec work** ("show us what you'd do for free") — bleed signal, no commitment
3. ❌ **Hourly billing** (kills leverage, invites scope creep, signals junior)
4. ❌ **Generic brand identity work** without operations context
5. ❌ **Social media management** or content production execution (not our category)
6. ❌ **Sub-€10k engagements** unless explicit lead-magnet purpose
7. ❌ **Sub-contracting for network agencies** at their margin (cannibalizes brand position)
8. ❌ **Awards submissions** that don't directly serve ICP visibility (Awwwards = peer, not buyer)

## 6. Pricing Defense

| Engagement | EN Price | PL Price | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Diagnostic | from €2,000 | from 8,000 PLN | Audit + roadmap — lead magnet for retainer |
| Brand Sprint | from €6,000 | from 25,000 PLN | Fixed scope, 5-day intensive |
| Enterprise Sprint | from €28,000 | from 120,000 PLN | Multi-location rollout, 6-week |
| Monthly Retainer | from €3,000/mo | from 12,000 PLN/mies. | Steady cadence, multi-track |
| Operating Partner | from €4,500/mo | from 18,000 PLN/mies. | Embedded, fractional COO/CDO |

**Pricing serves as filter, not menu.** If a prospect balks at €6k Sprint, they were never our ICP. Lower price = lower commitment = scope creep = burned hours.

**Risk reversal:** Money-back if no roadmap delivered. Used as anchor in objection handling, not as default offer.

## 8. The 1M PLN Path (12-month)

**Conservative model — what 1M PLN/year actually looks like:**

```
Retainer base       3 clients × €3,500/mo × 12 mo = €126,000  → ~540,000 PLN
Project Sprints     4 sprints × €8,000              = €32,000  → ~135,000 PLN
Enterprise Sprints  2 sprints × €30,000             = €60,000  → ~255,000 PLN
Operating Partner   1 client × €5,000/mo × 8 mo     = €40,000  → ~170,000 PLN
                                                                  ──────────
                                                       Total ≈   1,100,000 PLN
```

**That's:**
- 3 stable retainers
- 4 project sprints
- 2 enterprise wins
- 1 operating partner placement

**Per year. Solo. With AI leverage. With r3loop framework. With a site at 931/1000.**

The math works. The only missing variable is **distribution** — getting in front of enough COOs to close this volume.
