Process

Creative Operating System in 8 steps

How we build an operational creative delivery system for multi-location brands — step by step, from operational diagnosis to measurement and iteration.

Every creative operations project goes through the same 8 phases. We modify depth, not structure. Each phase has a clear contract: what we diagnose, what we design, what you get, what changes operationally, and which metric we improve.

Click through the phases from left to right to see how the system grows step by step — from diagnosing the current state to a steady rhythm of measurement and iteration.

00

Operational Discovery

We diagnose where requests, decisions, feedback, and production create friction — before we design anything.

What we diagnose

Request sources, their format, time from first email to finished asset, most common reasons for revisions.

What we design

A current-state map with 5–7 bottlenecks and their cost in hours per week.

What you get

A 1-page operational diagnosis with evidence and a recommended action sequence for the next 60 days.

What changes

Leadership sees the real bottlenecks, not their symptoms. Decisions stop being based solely on intuition.

Metric Time-to-cause for every recurring operational problem.
01

Demand Mapping

We map creative demand sources — who, what, how much, how often, in what format.

What we diagnose

Stakeholders, intake channels, request types, cyclicality, seasonality, local vs. central needs.

What we design

A request typology: strategic / urgent / local / cyclical / low-value — with clear definitions.

What you get

A demand map with volumes per stakeholder, a seasonal heatmap, and a list of requests to reject or defer.

What changes

Every new request can be classified in seconds, not hours. The backlog stops being an infinite list without priorities.

Metric % of requests with clear classification on intake (target 95%+).
02

Briefing System

We introduce briefing standards, Definition of Ready, and intake templates for every request type.

What we diagnose

The gap between what stakeholders write and what production needs. Most common reasons for follow-up questions.

What we design

Brief templates (3 variants: standard / local / express), Definition of Ready, intake form, AI brief assistant.

What you get

A template library, AI assistant with custom prompts, team training session, and a 90-day adoption playbook. AI supports input standardization, but decisions remain with people.

What changes

Briefs arrive complete on the first attempt. Most gaps surface before production starts, not during the second iteration.

Metric % of briefs meeting Definition of Ready on first submission (target 80%+).
03

Prioritization Logic

We define which requests go to production, which are deferred, and which are rejected — with clear rationale.

What we diagnose

Why "everything is ASAP" and what should actually be. Conflicts between strategy and operational pressure.

What we design

A 4-axis scoring rubric (urgency × strategic value × scope × dependencies) and a weekly triage cadence.

What you get

Prioritization rubric, decision rights map, weekly triage protocol, and a decision log for repeatability.

What changes

Only the right things go to production. Everything else gets a clear "defer" or "no" with rationale.

Metric % of requests with a "yes / defer / no" decision within 24h of intake.
04

Quality System

We turn subjective taste into benchmarks, QA rubrics, and production standards that can be delegated.

What we diagnose

Where quality depends on one person instead of a system. Recurring errors that seniors no longer consciously see.

What we design

A Creative Quality Playbook — senior judgment turned into rules. QA checklists per asset type, review workflow with RACI.

What you get

Creative Quality Playbook (50+ entries), QA checklists, review workflow, delegation training for mid+senior.

What changes

The team shares quality criteria. Seniors no longer need to be the only filter on every decision.

Metric % of work passing QA on first review (target 70%+).
05

Production Workflow

We define end-to-end workflows per asset type — who, when, on what input, with what handoff.

What we diagnose

Where handoffs lose context, time, and quality. Invisible micro-revisions that stretch cycles by weeks.

What we design

Workflow diagrams per asset type, RACI, handoff specs, integration with the team's existing tools.

What you get

Service blueprint, RACI per asset type, handoff specs, integration map with Asana / Notion / Figma / Slack.

What changes

Every project follows a known path. Ad-hoc stops being the default mode. A new team member understands the process in 1 day, not 2 months.

Metric Cycle time from brief in DoR to delivered, approved asset.
06

Approval & Governance

We define ownership, review paths, change request rules, and decision logs — so decisions are traceable, not political.

What we diagnose

Decision bottlenecks, unclear ownership, feedback loops that run the same content through three times.

What we design

Governance model, decision rights, escalation paths, change request process, decision log structure.

What you get

Governance playbook, RACI for approvals, decision log template, escalation protocol, conflict resolution path.

What changes

Decisions are owned, traceable, and don't bottleneck on one person. The system reduces dependency on individuals.

Metric Average decision time + decision reversal rate (both trending down).
07

Rollout & Optimization

We measure cycle time, revisions, brief quality, bottlenecks, and iterate the process — so the system stays current in a changing organization.

What we diagnose

Where the system starts leaking after rollout. Where the team reverts to old habits despite new rules.

What we design

Measurement system, iteration cadence, monthly review rituals, optimization playbook, AI ops layer.

What you get

KPI dashboard, monthly review structure, optimization playbook, quarterly governance review, and AI assistance setup.

What changes

The process stops being a one-time implementation — it has a rhythm of measurement, review, and iteration. Bottlenecks surface and get addressed in cadence, not after a breakdown.

Metric Trend of all metrics from phases 02–06 quarter-over-quarter.
Phase 01 of 08

After going through all 8 phases, the client's team receives operational artifacts they actually use in daily work: brief templates, Definition of Ready, prioritization rubric, Creative Quality Playbook, QA checklists, RACI, decision logs, production workflows, and a measurement dashboard.

What stays isn't a recommendation — it's a system.
